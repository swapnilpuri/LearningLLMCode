Companies Not Selected and Rationale:

1. Cohere:
Cohere is an exceptionally strong company, leading in enterprise-focused NLP and AI infrastructure. Its growth in ARR ($13 million in late 2023 to $100+ million in mid-2025), $7 billion valuation, and strategic alliances (AMD, Meta, etc.) demonstrate great execution and market validation. Cohere excels in technical customization, security, compliance, and international enterprise adoption. However, its primary market is B2B infrastructure rather than consumer disruption. The addressable market is huge, but the pace and scale of impact—particularly from a public perception or transformational standpoint—are not quite as dramatic as Perplexity AI’s. Its specialization makes it less likely to become a dominant platform comparable to the leading search/AI assistant paradigm shift.

2. Supabase:
Supabase posted remarkable developer-centric growth, expanding its user base from 1 million to 4.5 million and growing revenue from ~$15M (2024) to $70M (2025). Its viral popularity within the open-source and backend-as-a-service space makes it a foundational component for many startups and SMEs. However, Supabase’s market is more focused and at risk from hyper-scale cloud providers. While it is a tremendous success in developer tools and open-source software, the transformative, society-wide impact is not as pronounced as Perplexity’s in AI-powered search. The potential upside, while strong, is less outsized than an investment in the company leading a new generational shift in web search and assistant technology.
